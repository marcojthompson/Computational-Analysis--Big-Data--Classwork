'''Harpoon''' ('''Kodiak Noatak''') is a [[fictional character]] appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]]. He is a member of the [[Mutant (Marvel Comics)|mutant]] assassin team known as [[Marauders (comics)|Marauders]], who are employed by [[Mister Sinister]]. Little is known about Harpoon other than that he is [[Inuit]].

==Publication history==
{{expand-section|date=December 2020}}
Harpoon first appeared in ''Uncanny X-Men'' #210-211 (October–November 1986), and was created by [[Chris Claremont]], [[John Romita, Jr.]] and [[Dan Green (comics)|Dan Green]].

The character subsequently appears in ''Thor'' vol. 1 #373-374 (November–December 1986), ''X-Factor'' vol. 1 #10 (November 1986), ''Power Pack'' vol. 1 #27 (December 1986), ''Uncanny X-Men'' #213 (January 1987), 221-222 (September–October 1987), 240-241 (January–February 1989), ''X-Factor'' vol. 1 #38 (March 1989), ''Wolverine'' vol. 2 #10 (August 1989), ''X-Man'' #18 (August 1996), ''Cable & Machine Man Annual 1999'', ''Gambit'' #9 (October 1999), ''Weapon X'' #26 (September 2004), ''X-Men and Power Pack'' #4 (March 2006), ''New Avengers'' #18 (June 2006), ''X-Men'' #200-201 (August–September 2007), ''New X-Men'' #44-46 (January–March 2008), ''X-Factor'' #27 (March 2008), and ''X-Men'' #207 (March 2008).

Harpoon received an entry in the ''[[Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe|Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe Update '89]]'' #3.

==Fictional character biography==
Harpoon was one of the Marauders who attacked the [[X-Men|X-Man]] [[Warren Worthington III|Angel]] during the [[Mutant Massacre|Morlock Massacre]]. This is during the Marauders' murder of hundreds of [[Morlocks (comics)|Morlocks]]. Harpoon personally kills many, including the young mutant [[Cybelle (comics)|Cybelle]].{{Issue|date=December 2020}}

Harpoon pins Angel to the wall, but before he and his Marauder cohorts can slay him, [[Thor (Marvel Comics)|Thor]] intervenes and drives them off.{{issue|date=December 2020}} Harpoon hurls a harpoon at [[Rogue (comics)|Rogue]] while she is vulnerable after having her powers neutralized by the Marauder Scrambler. Kitty phases Rogue hoping Harpoon's weapon will pass harmlessly through her, but the spear is in an energy state and strikes them anyway, trapping Kitty Pryde in a ghost-like state.{{Issue|date=December 2020}}

Harpoon is thought to have been slain during the [[Inferno (Marvel Comics)|Inferno]] incident, but as often shown, Mister Sinister can clone his Marauders and bring them back at any time.{{Issue|date=December 2020}}

Harpoon is confirmed to be [[Decimation (comics)|depowered]] after the M-Day incident.<ref>''New Avengers'' #18</ref>

Harpoon appears with the rest of the Marauders.<ref>''X-Men'' vol. 2 #200</ref> Whether this is one of Sinister's clones has yet to be determined. To make up for his lack of power, he carries a weaponized harpoon, which Storm takes from him and wields herself.{{Issue|date=December 2020}} During the final battle on Muir Island, he is telepathically dispatched by Emma Frost.{{Issue|date=December 2020}}

==Powers and abilities==
Harpoon could charge his metal spears with his own bio-energy for various effects, such as shocking or stunning his opponents.  He was also exceptionally skilled in using his spears as weapons, both in melee combat and as thrown projectiles.

==Other versions==

===Age of Apocalypse===
In the reality known as the [[Age of Apocalypse]], Harpoon appears to be a member of Apocalypse's Infinite Patrol, the enforcers of En Sabuh Nur's purity as his swords of Justice.{{issue|date=December 2020}}

As an Infinite, Harpoon is seen briefly fighting the X-Men, but he is quickly defeated by Rogue. He begs her that he wants to just surrender but unemotionally, Rogue hits him away from her; Morph shape changes into a wall to stop the soldier's pace and keep him from ending up in the Pacific Ocean.<ref>''X-Men: Alpha''</ref>

==In other media==
Harpoon appears in the ''[[Wolverine and the X-Men (TV series)|Wolverine and the X-Men]]'' animated television series episode "eXcessive Force", voiced by [[Fred Tatasciore]].{{Citation needed|date=December 2020}} This version is a member of [[Mister Sinister]]'s [[Marauders (comics)|Marauders]]. Harpoon encounters the [[X-Men]] while they were searching for the missing [[Jean Grey]]. After determining he does not know where Grey is, the X-Men drop off Harpoon at a [[Mutant Response Division]] facility.
