'''Midnight Man''' is a fictional character appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]].

Midnight Man will make his live-action debut in ''[[Moon Knight (TV series)|Moon Knight]]'' (2022), on [[Disney+]] set in the [[Marvel Cinematic Universe]] with [[Gaspard Ulliel]] playing the character.

==Publication history==
Midnight Man first appeared in ''[[Moon Knight]]'' #3 (January 1981), and was created by [[Doug Moench]] and [[Bill Sienkiewicz]].

The character subsequently appears in ''Moon Knight'' #9–10 (July–August 1981).

==Fictional character biography==
Anton Mogart, as Midnight Man, is a costumed criminal who steals art treasures and valuable original manuscripts. As part of his schtick, he "always struck at midnight."  He was an enemy of [[Moon Knight]].

Mogart is presumed dead after his first encounter with Moon Knight after being shot by Marlene and falling off of his roof,<ref>''Moon Knight'' vol. 1 #3</ref> but he appears several issues later working with [[Raoul Bushman]].<ref>''Moon Knight'' vol. 1 #9</ref> Bushman betrays Midnight Man however, and leaves him and Moon Knight to drown in the flooding sewer system. Moon Knight manages to barely rescue himself and Midnight Man.<ref>''Moon Knight'' vol. 1 #10</ref>

Anton was the father of Jeff Wilde, later known as [[Midnight (Jeff Wilde)|Midnight]], who for a time served as a sidekick for the super hero Moon Knight. Later after a battle with the [[Secret Empire (comics)|Secret Empire]].<ref>''Moon Knight'' vol. 3 #19–21</ref> Jeff Wilde was transformed into a cyborg by the Secret Empire's scientists.{{issue|date=August 2012}} He went out on a killing spree to get Moon Knight's attention. Moon Knight beat Midnight Man in a brutal fight.{{issue|date=August 2012}}
